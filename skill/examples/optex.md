# Example — OPTEX Systems (the textbook pattern)

**One-line lesson:** cheap on *forward* earnings + a quantified, not-yet-priced
catalyst (rising backlog) + margin of safety → a clean fit; and sell the moment
the catalyst stops growing, even if you still like the company.

## How Malik reasoned it (Jan 2025)

Screened a sector by C/Z, hand-reviewed the cheapest names. Thesis: a **high,
quarter-by-quarter disclosed order backlog** guaranteeing future revenue and
profit growth — and *"tego nie było w wycenie"* (not yet in the price). Required
all of: non-one-off improvement, a growing order book, net cash surplus, and a
depressed price (a seller exiting) → *"duży margines bezpieczeństwa."*

Numbers as cited: C/Z ≈ **12** at purchase, but on his own forward-profit
estimate forward C/Z **< 10**; growing backlog; net cash. Outcome: **+100% in
~6 months**, then closed — *"backlog dalej nie rósł to zamknięcie — skończył się
pozytywny czynnik."*

## Verdict shape this should produce

- **thesis:** "Wysoki, rosnący backlog gwarantuje wzrost przychodów i zysków w
  kolejnych kwartałach; rynek jeszcze tego nie wycenia. Forward C/Z < 10 przy
  gotówce netto daje margines bezpieczeństwa."
- **catalysts:** `{type: order-book, description: rosnący portfel zamówień,
  horizon: 2–4 kwartały, priced_in: false}`
- **checklist highlights:** valuation vs own history — *spełnia* (forward C/Z
  <10 vs trailing ~12); profit quality — *spełnia* (backlog-driven, not one-off);
  balance sheet — *spełnia* (gotówka netto); catalyst — *spełnia* (quantified
  backlog).
- **one_off_risk:** low — improvement is order-driven, repeatable.
- **alignment_score:** high (80s) — all pillars present incl. a named catalyst.
- **verify_next:** "Czy backlog dalej rośnie? Gdy przestanie — teza wygasa
  (dyscyplina sprzedaży)."

**Teaching point:** the catalyst being *quantified and disclosed* is what makes
this the strongest version of the method — and the exit rule is part of the
thesis, not an afterthought.
